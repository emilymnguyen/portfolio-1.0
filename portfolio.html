My Portfolio

The task at hand was to create a simple portfolio website to accompany my resume. I needed to be able display my UI/UX and graphic design projects as well as my process for each one. I also wanted to express my minimal design style and provide easy access to my resume and platforms. To showcase my adaptive skill range, I took on the role of both the designer and developer to build my portfolio from the ground up.

Idea Generation

I began brainstorming by recognizing that the two basic features I needed to support were a gallery of my projects and a separate detailed page for each of them. Such simple content warranted a straightforward design. I also knew that I wanted a matching resume, and decided to work on that first to force myself to prioritize organization. My resume can be viewed here. I’m a big fan of flat, clean design, and settled on a grid design with generous whitespace.

Color Palette

For a clean look, I decided on a grayscale palette with one accent color. I kept my grays on the softer side to maintain a comfortable level of contrast between the text and the pure white background. I then picked out an exciting, energetic peach as my accent color to liven up the scheme.

Typography

For my body font, I chose Jaldi. Narrower and more rectangular than the typical sans serif, this typeface is subtly reminiscent of tech fonts. I wanted to represent that my focus is development, so this choice suits my branding perfectly. For my headlines, I decided to use Abel, a unique typeface that maintains my geometric theme.
